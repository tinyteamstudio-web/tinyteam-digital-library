Help & Guide

This guide helps you prepare your materials so TinyTeam can assemble your library smoothly and accurately. TinyTeam is a calm, human-handled, done-for-you process.

What to prepare

  • Library type: Family (FAM) or Community (COMM)
  • Design style: Heritage Classic, Modern Lineage, or Minimal Archive
  • People list: Names + optional notes (nickname, birth/death year, location)
  • Relationships: Parent/child, spouse/partner, key connections
  • Photos & documents (optional): photos, certificates, letters, scanned pages
  • Stories (optional): short stories, timelines, key memories

Tip: If you’re unsure, submit what you have first. TinyTeam will follow up gently if anything needs clarification.

How to describe relationships (simple examples)

  • Parent/Child: “Maria Santos is the mother of Jose Santos.”
  • Spouse/Partner: “Jose Santos is married to Ana Reyes.”
  • Siblings: “Jose Santos and Luisa Santos are siblings (same parents).”
  • Community: “Member A joined in 2002. Member B became a leader in 2010.”
  • Organization: “President → Vice President → Secretary → Members.”

You can submit in any format that is easy for you — text notes, lists, or a simple document.

Library size & expansion

Each library is designed to comfortably support up to 1,000 individuals. Larger families or extended branches can continue their archive by adding a connected library.

If you think your archive will exceed 1,000 people, submit anyway — TinyTeam will recommend the best expansion approach.

Photos, documents, and stories

If you choose the Photo & Stories add-on, TinyTeam can carefully place photos and documents alongside the people and timelines they belong to.

  • Clear file names help (example: “Grandpa-Jose-1968.jpg”)
  • Scans and photos are both accepted
  • If you have a timeline, include it — even rough notes help

If you have a large collection, TinyTeam will guide you on the easiest way to submit it.

Privacy & handling

TinyTeam libraries are assembled through a careful, human-handled process. Please avoid submitting highly sensitive information you do not want included in the archive.

Your completed library is delivered as a client-owned archive you can store independently.

What you will receive

  • Finished deliverables: a completed archive output (digital book + structured records)
  • Client-owned files: designed for long-term keeping and portability
  • Simple guidance: what’s inside and how to keep it safe

Note: TinyTeam delivers finished outputs — not reusable templates or editable source files.
