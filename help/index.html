Help & Guide

This guide helps you prepare your materials so TinyTeam can assemble your library smoothly and accurately. TinyTeam is a human-handled, done-for-you process.

What to prepare

  • Basic details: Your library type (Family / Community / Organization) and your preferred design style.
  • People list: Names (and optional notes like nicknames, birth/death year, location).
  • Relationships: Parent/child links, spouses/partners, and key connections.
  • Photos & documents (optional): Family photos, certificates, letters, awards, IDs, or scanned pages.
  • Stories (optional): Short written stories, timelines, or historical notes.

How to describe relationships (simple examples)

Use any format that is easy for you. Here are examples:

  • Parent/Child: “Maria Santos is the mother of Jose Santos.”
  • Spouse/Partner: “Jose Santos is married to Ana Reyes.”
  • Siblings: “Jose Santos and Luisa Santos are siblings (same parents).”
  • Community/Group: “Member A joined the group in 2002. Member B became a leader in 2010.”
  • Organization: “President → Vice President → Secretary → Members.”

If you are unsure, submit what you have — TinyTeam will follow up with gentle clarifying questions when needed.

Library size & expansion

Each library is designed to comfortably support up to 1,000 individuals. Larger families or extended branches can continue their archive by adding a connected library.

Photos, documents, and stories

If you choose the Photo & Document integration add-on, TinyTeam can carefully place photos and documents alongside the people and timelines they belong to.

  • Clear file names help (example: “Grandpa-Jose-1968.jpg”).
  • Scans and photos are both accepted.
  • If you have a timeline, include it — even rough notes help.

If you have a large collection, TinyTeam will guide you on the easiest way to submit it.

Privacy & handling

TinyTeam libraries are assembled through a careful, human-handled process. Please avoid submitting highly sensitive information you do not want included in the archive.

Your completed library is delivered as a client-owned archive you can keep and store independently.

What you will receive

  • Finished deliverables: A completed library output (example: a digital book PDF and/or a structured archive).
  • Client-owned archive: Files prepared for long-term keeping and portability.
  • Guidance: A simple README explaining what’s inside and how to keep it safe.

Note: TinyTeam applies design styles per project and delivers finished outputs — not reusable template source files.
