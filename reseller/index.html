Reseller Program

TinyTeam works with selected partners who help families, communities, and organizations discover digital preservation. Every request is still personally reviewed, validated, and delivered by TinyTeam.

Resellers do not access client projects, client data, or internal files — submissions remain official and private through TinyTeam.

At a glance

  • Commission: 20% flat commission (applies to validated orders: FAM / COMM / Add-On)
  • Model: referral + concierge (not a template resale license)
  • Tracking: client enters your reseller code in TinyTeam’s official Start form
  • Recording: credits are recorded after manual review + payment validation
  • Payout: manual payout after confirmation
  • Quality: approval required — TinyTeam is human-handled and curated

How it works

  1. Apply using the application link below.
  2. Get approved and receive your unique reseller code.
  3. Share TinyTeam through your own content, community, or funnel.
  4. Client submits their request on TinyTeam’s official Start page and enters your code.
  5. Commission is recorded after payment proof is confirmed and the request is validated.

Can resellers use funnels?

Yes — resellers may create educational or promotional funnels. However, TinyTeam remains the system owner and the official intake point.

  • Funnels may guide clients to TinyTeam’s official Start Your Library page.
  • Payments and submissions must happen through TinyTeam.
  • Resellers may not collect or store client family/community data.
  • Resellers may not promise reusable templates, editable source files, or instant approvals.

Partner rules (important)

To protect families and preserve quality, resellers must follow these rules:

  • No selling or sharing TinyTeam templates, internal files, or project folders.
  • No changing prices, offering discounts, or promising timelines.
  • No claiming “exclusive software ownership” — TinyTeam delivers finished archives.
  • No “eternal storage” promises — the client receives a portable, client-owned archive.
  • TinyTeam may revoke reseller status if the program is misrepresented.
